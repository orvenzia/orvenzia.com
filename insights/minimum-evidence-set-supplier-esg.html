The minimum evidence set SMEs need to pass enterprise ESG questionnaires

What buyers actually ask for and how to prepare a clean, credible evidence pack.

Enterprise buyers don’t need a 100‑page sustainability tome. They need a compact evidence pack that demonstrates policies, processes and data exist and are actively used. For SMEs, the fastest route to a 'pass' is a baseline set that procurement can review in minutes.

The core set typically includes five artefacts: a Health & Safety policy, an Environmental policy, a Code of Conduct (employee), a Supplier Code of Conduct, and a simple risk and escalation process note. Each document should be versioned, dated and assigned to an owner.

Alongside policies, buyers will ask for a short KPI overview. Keep it pragmatic: energy use, waste, incident rates and training coverage are most commonly requested for SMEs. Add a one‑page Scope 3 plan that outlines how you engage suppliers and which categories you will estimate first.

Evidence means traceability. Host documents in a version‑controlled repository, reference them in your report and use consistent file names. Include a simple ‘Evidence Index’ that lists each artefact, location and last update date. The easier it is to verify, the faster procurement moves.

Finally, write in buyer wording. Replace internal jargon with clear, formal English. Avoid marketing fluff and keep every paragraph actionable. If a buyer can map your pack to their questionnaire in under ten minutes, your odds of passing rise dramatically.
