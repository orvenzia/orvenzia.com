Procurement wording: how to answer ESG questions buyers actually ask

Write answers in the formats procurement expects and auditors can verify.

Most ESG responses fail not because the actions are wrong but because the wording is unclear. Procurement teams need answers that map to controls, processes and evidence. Replace narrative with short, verifiable statements.

Use a simple structure: policy reference, process summary, KPI or record, and a link to evidence. For example: 'Environmental Policy v1.3 (Jan 2025); monthly energy tracking; KPI: kWh per unit; evidence: energy.csv in repository.'

Avoid superlatives and marketing claims. Date every artefact and note ownership. Use consistent names so buyers can cross‑reference easily. When in doubt, prefer clarity over persuasion — procurement is not a marketing audience.
