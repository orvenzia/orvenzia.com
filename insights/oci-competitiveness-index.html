OCI: a practical competitiveness index for supplier‑readiness

How a simple 0–100 score drives action, clarity and faster buyer approvals.

The Orvenzia Competitiveness Index (OCI) converts supplier‑readiness into a 0–100 score so leaders can prioritise work. It removes ambiguity by weighting the five areas buyers care about: policies and governance, operations and risk, data and KPIs, supply chain and Scope 3, and reporting readiness.

An initial screening yields an OCI baseline. Scores above 70 indicate competitive suppliers that can proceed to buyer pitches. Scores between 50 and 69 are borderline — the focus should be on closing policy and evidence gaps. Below 50, start with a policy baseline before pursuing questionnaires.

OCI is not a certification. It is a practical decision tool for SMEs and buyers. Because it is focused on evidence and wording, OCI scores respond quickly to improvements and act as an internal KPI for procurement‑driven growth.

By publishing an OCI score and date in your buyer materials, you signal maturity and transparency. Buyers can see progress over time, and you can align internal teams around a common, practical target.
