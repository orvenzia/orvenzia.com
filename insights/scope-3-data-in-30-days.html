Scope 3 for SMEs: data you can realistically provide in 30 days

A pragmatic approach to upstream supplier data that satisfies enterprise buyers.

Scope 3 is daunting for SMEs because data sits outside your four walls. Buyers know this. They don’t expect perfect footprints; they expect a plan, prioritisation and credible proxies while you build supplier engagement.

Start with a category map. Identify the top spend categories, high‑risk materials and logistics lanes. Use supplier questionnaires to gather simple inputs: material mix, recycled content, certificates and any available footprints.

When data is missing, use recognised proxies and document your assumptions. The aim is to demonstrate intent and a repeatable process. Over time you can replace proxies with primary data. A clear 30/60/90‑day plan shows buyers that you will not stop at a one‑off estimate.

Create a short supplier communication pack. Explain why data is requested, how it will be used and the minimal fields required. Keep it achievable; response rates improve when you ask for less and follow up with a standard cadence.
