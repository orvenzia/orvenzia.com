Board‑ready vs. bureaucratic ESG: what C‑levels expect in 2025

Make ESG reports decision‑ready with concise metrics and a visible evidence trail.

Executives want ESG reports that inform decisions, not documents that catalogue activity. Board‑ready reporting uses short narratives, a stable KPI set and a visible trail to policies, data and processes.

For SMEs, the most effective approach is a one‑page dashboard with 6–8 KPIs and a quarterly note on changes, risks and opportunities. Keep the methodology section short and link to evidence rather than embedding pages of detail.

Tie ESG actions to commercial outcomes: win‑rate in RFPs, time‑to‑pass supplier screenings and quality incidents avoided. When leaders can see the commercial impact, ESG work receives the resources it needs.
